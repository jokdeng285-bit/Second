Our story

We built the app we wished existed

Spark was founded in 2026 by Jok Deng, as a platform for people looking for genuine connections, not just another dating app.

Our mission

Fewer hours on apps. More time on dates.

Most dating apps make money from your loneliness — the longer you stay single and scrolling, the more ads they serve. We set out to build the opposite: an app that succeeds when you succeed. Our metrics track couples formed, not time-in-app.

Every design choice we make is filtered through one question: does this help someone find a meaningful relationship faster, or does it just keep them on Spark longer?

🎯
Compatibility over clicks

We never A/B test addictive patterns. If a feature doesn't improve match quality, it doesn't ship.

🔒
Privacy by design

Your data is never sold. Your location is never stored beyond the session. You're the user, not the product.

🌍
Inclusive by default

Spark supports all orientations and relationship styles. Matching is opt-in, never algorithmic assumption.

📢
Honest metrics

We publish our success rates annually — including the ones that didn't improve. Accountability matters.

How we got here

2022
Founded in London

Founders Elise Carter and David Obi launch Spark from a Shoreditch co-working space with £180k in pre-seed funding.

2023
100,000 members

Spark reaches 100k members within 14 months and closes a £3.2m Series A. The interest-first algorithm launches.

2024
1 million members & Australia launch

Spark expands to Australia and hits 1 million verified members. First annual Transparency Report published.

2026
2.4 million members — and counting

340,000+ verified couples formed. Canada and Germany expansion announced for autumn 2026.
